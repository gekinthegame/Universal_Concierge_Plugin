# Acceptable Use & Copyright Notice

*Plain-language notice for users. This is a policy/notice, not legal advice — and
the platform deliberately **does not police** copyright (Decision 0031). It informs;
you are responsible.*

## What this tool is for
The Universal Concierge is for **your own memory and your own work**: capturing your
sessions, organizing your knowledge, collaborating, and **publishing things you created
or have the rights to share.** It is not a media locker or a file-distribution network.
By design it is **private-first** — nothing leaves your device or becomes public unless
*you* explicitly clear it for egress (Decision 0026 / 0030).

## Copyright — do not infringe
**Sharing or publishing content you do not own or have a license to is against the law and
against the rules of this tool.** Do not publish, pin, or distribute copyrighted media
(films, music, software, books, etc.) that isn't yours or properly licensed. When you
publish or clear something for egress, **you are the publisher and you are solely
responsible** for it.

## Public sharing is *not* anonymous
This is important and often misunderstood: this is **not a torrent network and not a place
to hide.** Because the network is content-addressed (IPFS), the moment you pin or serve
content **publicly**, your node advertises that content under your **peer identity and IP
address** (in DHT provider records and to anyone who fetches it). Public sharing is
**self-identifying and traceable.** If you use the public tier to infringe, you are doing
so in the open, attributable to you. Don't.

## What the platform does — and does not — do
- **It does not scan for, monitor, or enforce copyright.** There is no copyright detection,
  no automatic takedowns, no reporting of users. That responsibility is yours, and the
  matter is between you and any rightsholder.
- **It is private-by-default.** Your data is locked until you deliberately open it; the
  private node cannot serve the public network. You have to *choose* to make something
  public.

## Malware/safety is different (and *is* actively handled)
Don't confuse the two. **Malware is a threat to the *network's* health** — a malicious file
can harm other people's nodes — so the Guardian **actively** scans and **quarantines** unsafe
content to protect the mesh (herd immunity). That is the platform protecting *other nodes*,
not policing *your* legal compliance. Copyright, by contrast, harms no node's health; it is a
legal responsibility of the publisher, handled by this notice and the protocol's traceability
— not by built enforcement.

## In short
- Publish what you **own or are licensed** to share.
- **You are responsible** for what you make public.
- Public sharing is **attributable to you** — this is not a hiding place.
- The platform **informs, it does not police** copyright; malware (a network-health threat)
  it actively defends against.
